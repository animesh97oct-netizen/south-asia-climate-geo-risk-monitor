# South Asia Climate-Geopolitical Risk Monitor
**Animesh Banerjee | April 2026**

> Companion project to the [SARCI Index](https://github.com/animesh97oct-netizen/SARCI-index) — 
> applying the framework to a live crisis as it happened.

## What This Is
An original analytical brief tracking the collision of climate vulnerability 
and geopolitical risk across 8 South Asian countries — built in real time as 
the Bangladesh energy crisis unfolded in March 2026. The SARCI Index's HIGH 
rating for Bangladesh (3.17) was validated when QatarEnergy declared force 
majeure and diesel reserves fell to 14 days.

## Key Findings
- Bangladesh had zero energy buffer — 44% import dependency, 1.51% renewable 
  electricity, all supply routed through the Strait of Hormuz
- Afghanistan's 78% renewable figure is the most misleading statistic in South 
  Asia — it measures a grid too broken to deliver power
- Bhutan (SARCI 1.25, WGI 62.63) is South Asia's only net electricity exporter 
  — proof that governance and renewables create geopolitical insulation
